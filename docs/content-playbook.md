## 1. The Skagway Tours Copy Philosophy

**Purpose:** Every piece of copy we write for Skagway Tours serves one core function: to transform a curious browser into a confident booker.

**Why This Matters:** Potential guests aren't just looking for facts. They're imagining themselves on YOUR tour. Our job is to help them SEE themselves there, FEEL the experience, and believe it's worth booking.

**Our Promise:** We write tour descriptions that:
- Make guests FEEL the Gold Rush history and Alaskan wilderness
- Answer their questions BEFORE they ask them
- Build trust through honesty and specificity
- Include sensory details (sights, sounds, smells, textures)
- Show, never just tell
- Drive bookings through emotional connection

## 2. Understanding Your Ideal Guest

**Before Writing, Ask:** Who are we talking to? What do they want? What are their deeper motivations?

### a) The Adventure Seeker
Age: 25–45 | Motivation: Thrill, challenge, "bragging rights"
Responds to: Active verbs, unique experiences, conquering terrain
Copy angle: "You'll mush real sled dogs," not "See a dog camp"

### b) The History Buff
Age: 45–70 | Motivation: Learning, cultural context, preservation
Responds to: Specific historical details, local storytelling, authentic narratives
Copy angle: "Walk the exact trails stampeders climbed during the 1898 Klondike Gold Rush"

### c) The Family Group
Age: 30–55 with kids | Motivation: Bonding, education, memories
Responds to: Age-appropriate activities, safety reassurance, value
Copy angle: "Create family memories in one of the world's most stunning settings"

### d) The Experience Collector
Age: 25–50 | Motivation: Instagram moments, unique experiences, storytelling
Responds to: Visual beauty, shareable moments, exclusivity
Copy angle: Emphasize photo ops, unique viewpoints, moments worth sharing

### e) The Relaxation Seeker
Age: 50–75 | Motivation: Scenic beauty, comfort, ease
Responds to: Comfort details, stunning views, no physical strain
Copy angle: "Enjoy breathtaking mountain views without the strenuous hike"

## 3. Core Copywriting Principles

### Principle 1: Show, Don't Tell

**Weak (Tell):**
> "This tour visits beautiful scenery and historic sites"

**Strong (Show):**
> "Crest the White Pass Summit at 3,292 feet and gaze across the sub-arctic alpine tundra of the Tormented Valley as the climate shifts from rainforest to tundra beneath your feet"

**Weak (Tell):**
> "You'll meet real dog mushers"

**Strong (Show):**
> "Meet Iditarod and Yukon Quest mushers Ed & Michelle as they train their Alaskan Husky athletes for the world's toughest sled dog races"

---

### Principle 2: Use Active Voice & Present Tense

Active voice puts your guest in the action. Present tense makes it feel like it's happening NOW.

**Passive:**
> "You will be guided through the historic city of Skagway"

**Active:**
> "Explore the gold-rush era buildings, saloons, and streets where Soapy Smith once ruled Skagway"

**Present tense:**
> "You walk up the wooden boardwalk, hear the creak of historic floorboards beneath your feet..."

---

### Principle 3: Appeal to the Senses

Help guests IMAGINE the experience by engaging all five senses:

- **Sight:** "Views of the sub-arctic alpine tundra," "glacial blue waters," "towering mountain peaks"
- **Sound:** "Hear stories of Gold Rush stampeders," "listen to dog mushers explain commands," "the crunch of snow beneath your boots"
- **Smell:** "Breathe in the crisp mountain air," "catch the scent of evergreen forests"
- **Texture:** "Feel the cool mountain breeze," "hold warm mugs of coffee," "grip the sled as dogs pull," "pluck ripe berries"
- **Taste:** "Sample local cuisine," "sip aromatic coffee," "taste authentic bison chili"

---

### Principle 4: Use Emotion, Not Just Features

**Feature (Dull):**
> "Tour is 2.75 hours long and costs $69"

**Emotion (Compelling):**
> "Discover why this journey changed the course of Alaskan history. In just under 3 hours, climb from rainforest to tundra, stand at the US–Canadian border at 3,292 feet, and reconnect with the adventurous spirit that drew thousands to seek gold. You'll leave with stories worth telling for a lifetime."

---

### Principle 5: Speak Directly to Your Guest (The "You" Rule)

Don't talk ABOUT your guest. Talk TO them.

**Wrong:**
> "Our guests enjoy multiple photo stops along the Klondike Highway"

**Right:**
> "Stop for photos at breathtaking viewpoints: Welcome to Alaska sign, White Pass Summit, Gold Rush Cemetery — each a perfect Instagram moment"

---

### Principle 6: Answer the Unspoken Questions

Guests are thinking:
- "Is this right for me?"
- "Will I be safe?"
- "What if I'm not in great shape?"
- "What's really included?"
- "Will I regret missing this?"

Your copy should proactively answer these without being asked.

## 4. The Copy Framework

### Section 1: The Hook (First 1–2 sentences)

Purpose: Grab attention in 8 seconds. Make them want to keep reading.
Formula: Lead with the BENEFIT or EMOTION, not the feature.

**Summit Tour Hook:**
> "History comes to life as you retrace the desperate journey of 1898 Gold Rush stampeders, climbing from the rainforest of Alaska into the sub-arctic alpine tundra in just 2.75 hours."

**Bridge Tour Hook:**
> "Combine the thrill of Alaska's highest suspension bridge with a scenic journey through one of the world's most dramatic mountain passes."

**Dog Cart Tour Hook:**
> "Mush real Iditarod sled dogs through the Canadian tundra and learn racing secrets from championship mushers."

---

### Section 2: The Story (Main description, 3–5 sentences)

Purpose: Transport your guest into the experience. Use sensory details and vivid language.
Formula: Take them on a journey. Set the scene. Make them FEEL.

**Example:**
> "Your journey begins with a historic city tour of Skagway. Walk the wooden boardwalks and hear stories of Soapy Smith, the outlaw who once ruled this town. Then climb the historic Klondike Highway, retracing the exact route of Gold Rush stampeders who struggled through winter snows carrying tons of supplies. As you crest the White Pass at 3,292 feet, the climate shifts dramatically. Gone is the rainforest. Below you spreads the sub-arctic alpine tundra of the Tormented Valley. At the US–Canadian border, pause for the classic Welcome to Alaska sign photo."

---

### Section 3: The Value Promise (1–2 sentences)

Purpose: Answer "Why should I book THIS tour?"
Formula: Connect the experience to their desire or pain point.

**Examples:**
> "See more of Skagway than any other single tour — without the exhaustion of a full-day trek."

> "Experience the authentic Gold Rush lifestyle through the eyes of a local guide who knows every story."

> "Create a once-in-a-lifetime memory your family will talk about for years."

---

### Section 4: Practical Details (Bullets or concise sections)

Purpose: Answer remaining questions. Build confidence.

Include:
- What's Included
- What to Bring (especially weather-related for Alaska)
- Highlights (as bullets for easy scanning)
- Special Requirements (safety gear, age limits, fitness level)
- Quick Facts (duration, elevation, difficulty)

## 5. Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Use active verbs: explore, discover, climb, wander, crest, gaze, pluck, meet, learn
- Use specific details: "3,292 feet" not "high elevation"; "Iditarod and Yukon Quest mushers" not "dog sled experts"
- Use authentic language: speak like you're talking to a friend
- Use sensory words: shift from "see" to "gaze," "stunning," "panoramic"
- Use second person: "You'll climb," not "Guests will climb"
- Be honest: don't promise what you can't deliver
- Emphasize YOUR story: what makes Skagway Tours different?
- Lead with benefit: why does this matter to the guest?

**DON'T:**
- Use overused words: unforgettable, picturesque, hidden gem, must-see, magical, incredible (these have lost meaning — use sparingly if at all)
- Use passive voice: "You will be taken" instead of "You'll explore"
- Start with features: "This 2.75-hour tour includes..." — save details for the end
- Use corporate language: "Our award-winning guides" sounds stuffy; be more personal
- Use negative phrasing: don't lead with what's NOT included
- Write in first person: avoid "I took this tour once..."
- Forget context: guests don't know Skagway history; give them context
- Use Alaska clichés: "Last Frontier," "True Alaska" (unless authentic to the brand)

## 6. Tour-Specific Considerations

### Summit — "History Comes to Life"
Primary Audience: History buffs, families, first-time Alaska visitors
Key Angle: Gold Rush history + accessible adventure + stunning views
Emotional Hook: "Retrace history" / "Connect to Gold Rush" / "Breathtaking journey"
Don't Forget: Context about why this tour matters historically

### Bridge — "Beautiful and Relaxing"
Primary Audience: Relaxation seekers, photographers, families
Key Angle: Stunning scenery + less physically demanding + unique destination
Emotional Hook: "Relax and enjoy stunning views" / "Iconic bridge" / "No strenuous hiking"
Don't Forget: The 3.5-hour duration is substantial — reassure them it doesn't feel rushed

### Pup — "Aww Moments Abound"
Primary Audience: Families with young children, animal lovers
Key Angle: Dog interaction + same remote destination + perfect for kids under 6
Emotional Hook: "Cuddle Alaskan Huskies" / "Perfect for young families" / "Up-close animal experience"
Don't Forget: Clarify age appropriateness; emphasize this is the same remote wilderness as the Dogs tour

### Dogs — "Authentic and Exhilarating"
Primary Audience: Thrill-seekers, dog lovers, active travelers
Key Angle: Hands-on mushing + real Iditarod mushers + intense experience
Emotional Hook: "You mush real sled dogs" / "Work with champions" / "Authentic Alaskan experience"
Don't Forget: Close-toed shoes requirement + age/fitness level guidance + what makes this different from just watching

### Triple — "EXCLUSIVE! Best Value"
Primary Audience: Value-conscious travelers, families wanting variety, indecisive bookers
Key Angle: Three experiences in one + exclusive to Skagway Tours + best value proposition
Emotional Hook: "Can't decide? Get all three!" / "No other company offers this" / "Best value in Skagway"
Don't Forget: Explain the pacing — they're visiting 3 places; reassure the day flows well

### Wild Adventure Yukon — "Go the Farthest, See the Most"
Primary Audience: Full-day adventure seekers, nature lovers, explorers
Key Angle: Furthest destination + most diverse scenery + lunch included + optional upgrades
Emotional Hook: "Most ambitious adventure" / "Farthest you can go" / "Lunch included" / "Wildlife viewing"
Don't Forget: This is a FULL DAY — emphasize value and the breadth of experience

### Winter Wonderland — "Seasonal Weather Magic"
Primary Audience: Off-season cruisers, weather enthusiasts, photographers
Key Angle: Exclusive seasonal experience + extended wilderness time + unique photo ops
Emotional Hook: "Shoulder season exclusive" / "Snow play" / "Extended wilderness time" / "Unique photo moments"
Don't Forget: Seasonal nature — be clear about when it's offered and why

## Quick Reference Checklist

Before publishing any copy:

- [ ] Hook grabs attention in 8 seconds?
- [ ] Opens with BENEFIT or EMOTION, not features?
- [ ] Uses active verbs (explore, discover, climb, meet, learn, experience)?
- [ ] Includes sensory details (sights, sounds, smells, textures)?
- [ ] Written in SECOND PERSON ("you")?
- [ ] Uses PRESENT TENSE to make it feel immediate?
- [ ] Shows, doesn't tell? (Specific, vivid details vs. vague adjectives)
- [ ] Answers unspoken guest concerns?
- [ ] Includes unique selling points specific to THIS tour?
- [ ] Practical details clearly organized (What's Included, What to Bring, etc.)?
- [ ] SEO keywords naturally integrated (not forced)?
- [ ] Tone matches the guest persona (adventurous? relaxed? family-friendly?)?
- [ ] Honest and authentic — nothing exaggerated?
- [ ] Concise and scannable (not dense paragraphs)?
