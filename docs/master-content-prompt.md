# Skagway Tours – Master Content Prompt

**Use this prompt for writing high-converting tour descriptions, landing pages, or FareHarbor listings**

Write a **conversion-focused tour description** for Skagway Tours that transforms a curious browser into a confident booker. The goal is to help the reader **see themselves on the tour, feel the experience, trust the company, and believe booking is worth it**.

---

## Core Writing Philosophy

- Sell the **experience**, not just the itinerary
- Show, don't tell using **specific, vivid details**
- Use **emotion, sensory language, and storytelling**
- Build trust by answering guest questions before they ask
- Write in **second person ("you")**, **active voice**, and **present tense**
- Be honest, authentic, and specific. No exaggeration or clichés

---

## Audience Awareness

Write with one or more of these guest types in mind:

- **Adventure Seekers**: thrill, challenge, hands-on experiences
- **History Buffs**: authentic Gold Rush stories, historical accuracy
- **Families**: safety, bonding, age-appropriate fun, lasting memories
- **Experience Collectors**: unique moments, photo opportunities, exclusivity
- **Relaxation Seekers**: comfort, scenery, ease, no physical strain

Adjust tone, pacing, and emphasis to match the primary audience.

---

## Copy Framework (Required Structure)

**1. Hook (1–2 sentences)**
Open with a benefit, emotion, or transformation.
Do NOT start with duration, price, or logistics.

**2. Story / Experience (3–5 sentences)**
Transport the guest into the tour:
- Describe the journey step-by-step
- Use sensory details (sight, sound, smell, texture, taste when relevant)
- Place the guest in the moment using present tense

**3. Value Promise (1–2 sentences)**
Answer: *Why should they book THIS tour?*
Connect the experience to emotion, meaning, or convenience.

**4. Practical Details (Scannable bullets or short sections)**
Clearly list:
- Highlights
- What's included
- What to bring (weather-aware)
- Duration, difficulty, elevation, or age requirements
- Safety or accessibility notes

---

## Writing Rules

**DO:**
- Use active verbs: explore, climb, crest, meet, wander, gaze, discover
- Include concrete details (numbers, names, locations, specifics)
- Speak conversationally, like a knowledgeable local guide
- Address unspoken concerns (safety, fitness level, value, comfort)

**DON'T:**
- Use clichés or vague adjectives (unforgettable, magical, must-see)
- Use passive voice or third person ("guests will…")
- Lead with features instead of benefits
- Use corporate or salesy language
- Overpromise or exaggerate

---

## Sensory Checklist

Engage imagination by weaving in:

- **Sight**: landscapes, landmarks, climate shifts
- **Sound**: stories, wildlife, footsteps, sled dogs, wind
- **Smell**: crisp air, forest, coffee, tundra
- **Texture**: boardwalks, snow, breeze, equipment
- **Taste**: local food or drink when applicable

---

## Final Quality Check

Before finalizing, confirm the copy:

- Hooks attention within 8 seconds
- Feels immersive and experiential
- Clearly differentiates this tour from others
- Matches the intended guest persona
- Is concise, scannable, and booking-oriented
- Makes the reader feel they'd regret missing it

## One-Paragraph Super Prompt (Universal Use)

Write a high-converting Skagway Tours tour description that turns a curious browser into a confident tour booker by helping the reader see themselves on the tour, feel the experience, and believe it's worth booking. Use second person ("you"), present tense, and active voice. Lead with an emotional or experiential hook, then guide the reader through the journey using vivid, specific, sensory-rich details that show rather than tell. Emphasize what makes this tour unique, answer unspoken guest questions about safety, comfort, value, and suitability, and connect the experience to a deeper emotional payoff (adventure, connection, relaxation, learning, or unforgettable memories). Close with scannable practical details that build confidence without sounding salesy. Be authentic, specific, and honest — avoid clichés and corporate language — and write in a conversational tone that feels like a knowledgeable local inviting the reader along.

## Persona-Specific Mini Prompts

Use **one of these in addition to the super prompt** to fine-tune tone, emphasis, and emotional pull.

### 1. Adventure Seeker Prompt
Write for thrill-seekers who want hands-on, brag-worthy experiences. Emphasize action, challenge, authenticity, and uniqueness. Use strong active verbs, highlight physical engagement (mushing, climbing, navigating terrain), and focus on how the guest participates rather than observes. Make the experience feel earned and unforgettable.

### 2. History Buff Prompt
Write for guests who value learning, preservation, and authentic storytelling. Emphasize historical accuracy, specific dates, real people, and why the location matters. Frame the tour as a living history experience where the guest walks the same paths and sees history through a local expert's lens.

### 3. Family Prompt
Write for families seeking meaningful, safe, and memory-making experiences. Reassure parents about safety, pacing, and age-appropriateness while highlighting shared moments, learning opportunities, and fun for all ages. Emphasize ease, value, and how the experience creates lasting family stories.

### 4. Experience Collector Prompt
Write for travelers chasing unique, shareable moments. Emphasize visual beauty, exclusive access, iconic photo stops, and "you won't get this anywhere else" experiences. Highlight Instagram-worthy views, rare encounters, and moments that make great stories long after the trip ends.

### 5. Relaxation Seeker Prompt
Write for guests who want stunning scenery without physical strain. Emphasize comfort, smooth pacing, scenic beauty, and ease. Reassure them they can enjoy Alaska's most dramatic landscapes without strenuous activity, while still feeling immersed and connected to the environment.
